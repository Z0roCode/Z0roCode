## About Me

I'm Zorium, and I run Z0roCode.

Before I build anything, I study why certain websites work. I look at how real estate sites turn browsers into calls, how clinics make patients comfortable enough to book online, how a cafe menu gets someone to actually place an order instead of closing the tab. Then I apply what I find.

Design and code are only half the job. The other half is understanding the business behind the website: who it's trying to reach, what it needs people to do, and what's currently getting in the way of that.

I enjoy that part more than most people expect. Finding the pattern in a site that converts well, then figuring out how to bring that same clarity to a project I'm building, is what makes this work interesting to me.

<br>

## Who I Build For

Every project starts the same way: understanding the business before touching any code.

That means looking at the target audience, checking what competitors are doing well and where they fall short, and mapping out the actual journey a visitor takes from landing on the page to taking action.

From there, the focus is on:

- Simplifying navigation so people don't have to think about where to go next
- Building trust quickly, since most visitors decide how credible a site is within seconds
- Balancing performance with visual polish, because a beautiful site that loads slowly loses people anyway
- Keeping every section purposeful, nothing added just to fill space
- Designing with intent instead of chasing trends
- Reducing friction wherever it shows up, in forms, in menus, in checkout flows
- Making the path to action feel natural rather than forced

I work across a few industries where this matters most: real estate, healthcare and clinics, cafes and restaurants, salons, and professional services. Each one has a different idea of what "trust" and "action" look like, and the site needs to reflect that.
